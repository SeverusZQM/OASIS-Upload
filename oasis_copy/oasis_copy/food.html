A Universal Formula for lunch box combinations :

Vegetables & Fruit + Protein + Carbs + Seasonings

Vegetables like lettuce, broccoli, and carrots can provide abundant dietary fiber, vitamins, and minerals. You can choose stir-frying or cold tossing as the cooking method, which can not only retain the nutrients but also enhance the textural layers.

Fruits like blueberries and pineapples are rich in vitamin C and antioxidants; bananas and oranges are good sources of potassium and B vitamins; dragonfruit and apples can provide abundant dietary fiber. Pairing with fruit salad can make the taste more refreshing and crisp.

Salmon is rich in omega-3 fatty acids, which are beneficial for cardiovascular health; eggs and lean meat provide high-quality protein, which helps with muscle synthesis and repair, fish and shrimp are also excellent sources of marine-based protein.

Salmon can be simply pan-fried or roasted to maintain the tender and juicy texture. Eggs can be fried, boiled, or scrambled according to personal preference, and can also be made into leaf-tea eggs or salted eggs to retain a rich and thick mouthfeel. Beef and chicken can be sliced or minced, stir-fried into meat cubes or meat sauce, and paired with other ingredients. Fish fillets and shrimp are suitable for steaming or stir-frying.

Complex carbohydrates like mixed grain rice and quinoa can help stabilize blood sugar levels for a longer duration. Sweet potatoes and pumpkins are rich in nutrients like Vitamin A. These ingredients not only provide energy, but also increase the feeling of fullness in the meals.

Ingredients like olive oil, flaxseed oil, salt, black pepper, and chopped parsley can be used for seasoning. Proper seasoning can help accentuate the natural flavors of the ingredients and enhance the overall taste.

Seasonal Adjustments

Spring: Seasonal vegetables like chives and young bamboo shoots can be stir-fried or tossed in a cold dish to maintain a fresh taste. Fruits like strawberries and bayberries can be good choices.

Summer: Seasonal vegetables like eggplant and corn can be good options. Refreshing and juicy fruits like watermelon and pomelo can be selected.

Autumn: Seasonal vegetables like yam and chestnuts can be paired, roasted or stewed to enhance the soft and sweet mouthfeel. Autumn pears can be a good fruit choice.

Winter: Seasonal vegetables like endives and Chinese broccoli can be paired with warm and comforting stewing or baking methods. Citrus fruits like oranges and pomelos can be good choices.
